Your PostgreSQL database contains the following tables:

IMPORTANT — three-tier data model:
  1. 'savant_*' tables (28-37) — two different coverage windows:
     - Counting/ratio tables (savant_batting_traditional, savant_batting_ratios,
       savant_pitching_traditional, savant_pitching_ratios) hold ONLY the current,
       in-progress season, updated daily. Use these first for any player-season
       counting/rate query. Do not assume a fixed cutover year (e.g. "2025") — the
       boundary moves forward every season; check for rows instead.
     - Statcast-exclusive tables (savant_batting_expected/physics/discipline,
       savant_pitching_expected/physics/discipline) hold full history from 2015
       onward (Statcast's first full tracking season) in addition to the current
       season. Query these directly for any season 2015+ — no Lahman/FanGraphs
       fallback exists for these columns, they exist nowhere else in this database.
  2. Lahman tables (people, batting, pitching, teams, etc.) are the fallback for any
     season Savant's counting/ratio tables don't have — historical counting stats
     AND basic rate stats (AVG/OBP/SLG/ERA — compute these from Lahman's raw counts,
     e.g. h::numeric/ab). Lahman is also the ONLY source for awards, Hall of Fame
     voting, postseason stats, managers, and standings, at any season.
  3. FanGraphs tables 'fangraphs_batting_advanced' (fba) / 'fangraphs_pitching_advanced'
     (fpa) are a FROZEN historical archive — no new FanGraphs data can be loaded
     anymore, ever, regardless of what MAX(season) shows in these tables (a stale
     partial current-season row may exist and does NOT mean this data is live).
     These two tables are the ONLY source for WAR, wOBA, wRC+, FIP, and xFIP — no
     other table has them. Do not use FanGraphs for counting stats, basic rates, or
     anything Lahman/Savant can already answer. All other 'fangraphs_*' tables
     (fbl, fpl, fbr, fpr, fpd, fbb, fbf, fpp, fpb) are legacy/deprecated — do not
     use them in new queries even though they still exist in the database.

21. awardsplayers
Description: Awards given to players

playerid (text) – Player ID.
awardid (text) – Award name. Real values are full names, not abbreviations — do
  not guess a short form or invent a LIKE pattern from the question's wording
  alone. Confirmed real values for commonly-asked awards:
    MVP -> 'Most Valuable Player' (NOT 'MVP' — that bare string never appears;
      ALCS MVP / NLCS MVP / World Series MVP / All-Star Game MVP are separate
      postseason/exhibition awards that do say "MVP" literally).
    Cy Young / Rookie of the Year / Gold Glove / Silver Slugger -> 'Cy Young
      Award' / 'Rookie of the Year' / 'Gold Glove' / 'Silver Slugger' already
      match natural phrasing closely; no translation needed.
    Reliever of the Year -> 'Reliever of the Year Award' for 1976-2023 and
      2025; the year 2024 specifically used the bare 'Reliever of the Year'
      string instead (a one-year data inconsistency). Match with awardid ILIKE
      '%reliever of the year%' rather than an exact string, or 2024's winners
      will be missed. 'TSN Reliever of the Year' is a separate, genuinely
      different award from The Sporting News, not an alternate name.
    Comeback Player of the Year -> THREE distinct real awards exist:
      'Comeback Player of the Year' (MLB's own, 2005-present), 'MLB Players
      Choice Comeback Player' (players'-choice vote, 1997-2024), and 'TSN
      Comeback Player of the Year' (The Sporting News, 1965-present). If the
      question doesn't name a specific voting body, default to the plain
      'Comeback Player of the Year'.
    Triple Crown -> 'Triple Crown' for batting (AVG/HR/RBI), 'Pitching Triple
      Crown' for pitching (W/ERA/SO) — cleanly separated in the data; choose
      based on whether the question is about a hitter or a pitcher.
  This table has ~80 distinct awardid values total, mostly niche one-off and
  "This Year in Baseball [X]"/"TSN [X]" awards not covered above — when a
  question names an award not listed here, prefer awardid ILIKE '%keyword%'
  over guessing an exact string.
yearid (integer) – Year awarded.
lgid (text) – League.
tie (text) – Tie flag.
notes (text) – Notes.
